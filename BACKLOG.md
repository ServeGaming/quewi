**Note on scope and sequencing.** This is roughly a 9–15 month effort for a small team: the "Top picks" plus the high-priority items in each group are about 2–3 months and turn the demo-grade subsystems into reliable ones (audio effects/persistence, video transport+black-hold, group hierarchy, arm UI, Art-Net/DMX-USB, code signing). Sequence in three waves. Wave 1 — correctness and persistence: make edits survive reload (audio regions, undo gaps, patch/show-file persistence) and ship the signed, green-gated release pipeline, since these de-risk everything downstream. Wave 2 — parity depth per subsystem: real-time audio effects + matrix mixer, the video transport/scrubber/fade/black-hold/transition cluster, lighting outputs + fixture library + masters, and the OSC/MIDI/MSC control surface (typed-arg editor, expanded triggers, MSC receive, timecode). Wave 3 — the XL differentiators and breadth (GPU compositing, projection-mapping depth, Spout/Syphon/NDI, scripting, plugin API, multi-operator sync, console-specific integrations), which are large and largely independent, so schedule them by demand rather than as a blocking line. Throughout, fold in the low-effort accessibility and UX-workspace fixes (arm UI, rebindable shortcuts, accessible names, context-menu reorder, doc-comment corrections) as filler between the larger pushes.
